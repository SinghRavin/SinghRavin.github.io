Ravi Singh

Hi! I am Ravi, a PhD candidate in the Division of Biostatistics, Department of Population Health Sciences, at the University of Utah. Before starting my PhD program, I completed my Masters in Applied Statistics at Bowling Green State University. I received my dual BS-MS degree in mathematics from the School of Mathematics, Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, Thiruvananthapuram, India.

My current research interest lies in causal inference and applying advanced methods in statistics and machine learning to answer relevant questions in biomedical research. My thesis work focuses on estimating heterogeneous treatment effects using state-of-the-art methods such as the R-learner, targeted maximum likelihood estimation, and risk- and effect-modeling approaches. I apply these methods to randomized controlled trials as well as observational studies using electronic health record data, where the observational analyses are guided by the principles of target trial emulation. I also have a strong passion for teaching.

You can find me on GitHub, Google Scholar, LinkedIn, or email me at ravinder.singh@utah.edu. A copy of my CV is available here.
